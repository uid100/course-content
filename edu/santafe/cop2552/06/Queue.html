Overview

A queue is an example of a First-In, First-Out (FIFO) system. Imagine people standing in line to buy food at the cafeteria; the first person in line (First-In) is the first person to get their food (First-Out).

Image from: http://tutorials.jenkov.com/java-collections/queue.html

You can try a queue here: Queue (Linked List implementation) (https://www.cs.usfca.edu/~galles/visualization/QueueLL.html)

Use Cases

Use a queue when you have many agents adding to a collection, but only one agent that "consumes" from a collection. In Java, the Queue interface is used for implementation.
