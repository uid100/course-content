Formatting output using f-strings in Python

F-strings are a convenient and powerful feature in Python for formatting strings dynamically. They provide a concise and readable way to embed expressions inside string literals, making string formatting more intuitive and efficient.

What are F-strings?

An F-string is a string literal prefixed with 'f' or 'F'. Within an F-string, expressions enclosed in curly braces '{ }' are evaluated at runtime, and their results are embedded into the string.

How to Use F-strings

To use F-strings, simply prefix a string literal with 'f' or 'F' and place expressions inside curly braces within the string. For example:

        
            name = "Alice"
            age = 30
            print(f"Hello, my name is {name} and I am {age} years old.")

This will output: Hello, my name is Alice and I am 30 years old.

History of F-strings

F-strings were introduced in Python 3.6, which was released in December 2016. Since then, they have become increasingly popular due to their simplicity and efficiency in string formatting.

With their ease of use and readability, F-strings have become a preferred method for string interpolation in Python, replacing older formatting methods like %-formatting and str.format().

Overall, F-strings are a valuable addition to Python's arsenal of string manipulation tools, offering a more elegant and concise syntax for dynamic string formatting.
