World Health Organization (WHO)

Mission

The World Health Organization (WHO) aims to promote and protect global public health.

Its core mission includes

  • Providing Leadership
    in matters of global health.
  • Setting Norms and Standards
    for healthcare practices.
  • Monitoring Health Trends
    and assessing global health situations.
  • Providing Technical Assistance
    to countries to strengthen healthcare systems.
  • Coordinating International Responses
    to health emergencies, such as pandemics and disasters.
  • Advancing Equity
    in access to health services, medicines, and disease prevention.

Categories of Data WHO Collects

WHO collects and monitors a wide range of health-related data (partial)

  • Epidemiological Data
    Incidence, prevalence, and mortality rates for diseases (e.g., COVID-19, malaria, tuberculosis).
  • Health Systems Data
    Healthcare infrastructure, availability of health services, and human resources (e.g., doctors, nurses).
  • Environmental Health Data
    Air and water quality, sanitation, and environmental risks to health.
  • Health Policy and Economic Data
    Information on healthcare funding, expenditures, and policy effectiveness.
  • Demographic and Social Data
    Population health metrics, including life expectancy, maternal and child health, and nutrition.
  • Vaccination and Immunization Data
    Coverage of vaccination programs, vaccine safety, and efficacy.
  • Global Health Emergency Data
    Real-time data on outbreaks, public health emergencies, and disaster response.

Data Collection Methods

WHO collects data through various mechanisms

  • Member States Reporting
    Countries report health data and statistics to WHO regularly.
  • Health Surveys
    WHO conducts global and national health surveys in collaboration with member states.
  • Research Networks and Collaborations
    WHO partners with universities, research institutions, and other international health organizations.
  • Field Assessments
    WHO sends experts to conduct on-ground assessments during health crises or special investigations.
  • Satellite and Geospatial Data
    For monitoring environmental factors and emergencies.

Data Sharing

WHO shares data through:

  • Global Health Observatories
    Online platforms like the Global Health Observatory (GHO) provide access to health-related statistics.
  • Public Reports and Bulletins
    WHO regularly publishes health reports, situation updates, and technical documents for the public, researchers, and policymakers.
  • Collaborations with Governments
    Data shared with national health departments to guide policy-making and international cooperation.
  • Partnerships with NGOs and International Organizations
    WHO collaborates with groups like UNICEF, the World Bank, and others to coordinate data use for global health initiatives.

Data is disseminated in a transparent, standardized manner, ensuring that it can be used for health policy, research, and global health surveillance.
