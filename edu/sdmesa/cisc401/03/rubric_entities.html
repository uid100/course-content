Healthcare Database Design Discussion Rubric

Total Points: 50

Part 1: Identify Key Entities, Attributes, and Relationships (35 Points)
Criteria Exemplary (35-31 pts) Proficient (30-26 pts) Needs Improvement (25-21 pts) Insufficient (20-0 pts)
Entity Identification (10 pts) Clearly identifies at least 5 relevant and distinct entities essential for healthcare database design. Identifies 4-5 relevant entities with minor omissions or redundancies. Identifies fewer than 4 entities, or some are irrelevant. Entities are missing or irrelevant to healthcare data.
Attribute Definition (15 pts) Each entity includes comprehensive, clearly defined key attributes with accurate data types. Most entities include appropriate attributes, but a few are unclear or missing data types. Attributes are missing, incomplete, or lack clarity in more than one entity. Attributes are vague, missing, or unrelated to the entities.
Relationships (10 pts) Relationships between entities are clearly and accurately described with detailed connections. Relationships are identified but lack detail or have minor inaccuracies. Relationships are vague or partially inaccurate in key areas. Relationships are missing, unclear, or irrelevant.
Part 2: Collaboration (15 Points)
Criteria Exemplary (15-13 pts) Proficient (12-10 pts) Needs Improvement (9-7 pts) Insufficient (6-0 pts)
Peer Feedback (15 pts) Provides thoughtful, constructive feedback on at least 3 peers' work. Suggestions enhance their designs and include specific examples. Reviews 2-3 peers' work with constructive feedback. Suggestions are general or lack examples. Reviews fewer than 2 peers' work or feedback is vague or lacks helpful suggestions. Fails to review peers' work or feedback is irrelevant or superficial.
