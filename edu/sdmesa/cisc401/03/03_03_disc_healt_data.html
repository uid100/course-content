Healthcare Data

Healthcare Information Design

Objective

Consider any aspect of the healthcare industry, from patient care, medical billing, facilities management, compliance, or research. The data generated and managed in healthcare is vast, complex, and critical to the delivery of quality care. Designing databases to manage this data is a crucial aspect of healthcare information systems.

As healthcare professionals, understanding how data is organized, stored, and managed in a healthcare database is crucial. This discussion will help you identify key data entities, their associated fields and attributes, and the relationships between them that are essential for effective healthcare information management.

Instructions

Part 1. Identify Key Entities, Attributes and Relationships (35 pts)

In the context of a database, an entity is an object, concept, or thing in the real world that can be distinctly identified and stored as a record in a database. Entities are typically represented by tables in a relational database, where each row in the table corresponds to a specific instance of the entity.

Consider any aspect of healthcare delivery, such as patient care, treatment management, billing, and public health reporting. Identify at least five entities (e.g., Patient, Doctor, Appointment, Medication, Insurance, LabTest) that would be crucial to managing healthcare information in a database.

For each entity, define the key attributes or fields that are essential for representing and managing the data associated with that entity.

Attributes are the specific pieces of information that describe or characterize an entity. For example, the Patient entity might have attributes such as Patient ID, Name, Date of Birth, primary care provider.

Finally, identify and describe the relationships between these entities. Relationships define how the entities are connected or related to each other in the database. For example, a Patient entity may have a relationship with an Appointment entity, indicating that a patient can have multiple appointments. The Appointment entity may have a relationship with a Doctor entity, indicating that a doctor can have multiple appointments.

Format your response as a list or table, clearly identifying the entities, their attributes and data types, and the relationships between them.

Part 2. Collaboration (15 pts)

Review the responses of at least three of your peers. Provide constructive feedback on their entity identification, attribute definition, and relationship description. Offer suggestions for improvement or additional insights that could enhance their database design, or where any of your entities or attributes might be related to theirs.

Example

Here is an example of 4 non-healthcare-related entities, attributes, and relationships to help you get started: 

    Songs
    Attribute Name      |  Data Type    |  Description
    -----------------------------------------------------
     SongID             |  INT          |  Unique identifier for each song.
     Title              |  VARCHAR(255) |  Title of the song.
     Duration           |  TIME         |  Length of the song.
     ReleaseDate        |  DATE         |  Date the song was released.
     ArtistID           |  INT          |  Link to Artists table.
     GenreID            |  INT          |  Link to Genres table.
     RecordingCompanyID |  INT          |  Link to RecordingCompanies table.

    Artists
    Attribute Name      |  Data Type    |  Description
    -----------------------------------------------------
     ArtistID           |  INT          |  Unique identifier for each artist.    
     Name               |  VARCHAR(255) |  Name of the artist.
     BirthDate          |  DATE         |  Date of birth of the artist.
     Country            |  VARCHAR(255) |  Country of origin.
     RecordingCompanyID |  INT          |  Link to RecordingCompanies table.

    Recording Companies
    Attribute Name      |  Data Type    |  Description
    -----------------------------------------------------
     RecordingCompanyID |  INT          |  Unique identifier for each company.
     CompanyName        |  VARCHAR(255) |  Name of the recording company.
     FoundedDate        |  DATE         |  Date the company was founded.
     Country            |  VARCHAR(255) |  Country where the company is based.

    Genres
    Attribute Name      |  Data Type    |  Description
    -----------------------------------------------------
     GenreID            |  INT          |  Unique identifier for each genre.
     GenreName          |  VARCHAR(255) |  Name of the music genre.  
     Description        |  TEXT         |  Description of the genre.

Relationships:

  • Songs → Artists:
    Each song is associated with one artist (ArtistID is a foreign key in the Songs table pointing to ArtistID in the Artists table). An artist can have multiple songs (One-to-Many relationship
  • Songs → Genres:
    Each song belongs to one genre (GenreID is a foreign key in the Songs table pointing to GenreID in the Genres table). A genre can have multiple songs (One-to-Many relationship
  • Songs → Recording Companies:
    Each song is produced by one recording company (RecordingCompanyID is a foreign key in the Songs table pointing to RecordingCompanyID in the RecordingCompanies table). A recording company can produce multiple songs (One-to-Many relationship
  • Artists → Recording Companies:
    Each artist can be signed to one recording company (RecordingCompanyID is a foreign key in the Artists table pointing to RecordingCompanyID in the RecordingCompanies table). A recording company can have multiple artists (One-to-Many relationship).
